About WHWeb

World Heritage Web

place and period in world history

Designated heritage sites—from the monuments, city centres and cultural landscapes on UNESCO's World Heritage List to simple roadside markers—can serve as waypoints in interactive historical narratives or as a lattice for exploring historical connections.

Cultural heritage is found not only in such large-scale places, but in distinctive cultural practices and associated artifacts.

Joining concepts of place and period, we are developing a novel geographical computing framework to support both the exploration of web-based historical resources and the authoring of interactive histories.

World Heritage Web is building scholarly and educational web applications. Enabling technologies developed collaboratively along the way are being made freely available (presently, at the Orbis Initiative and Topotime GitHub repositories)
