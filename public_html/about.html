A brief history

World Heritage Web, Inc. was founded in 2003 as a California 501(c)(3) non-profit corporation, by Karl Grossner, in order to provide an institutional base for developing digital historical atlases for educators, students, and the general public. In its first two years we built prototype applications for presenting historical narratives using designated cultural heritage sites as navigational waypoints, and pitched UNESCO and other organizations for support. The organization then went on hiatus for the next decade as Karl first earned a PhD in geography at UC Santa Barbara ("Representing Historical Knowledge in Geographic Information Systems"), then built several elaborate interactive scholarly works over a 4 1/2 year period at Stanford University. This has laid the groundwork for future efforts (beginning in 2016) at building geo-historical information systems that are far more research-oriented, and grounded in geographic and geographic information science theory.
